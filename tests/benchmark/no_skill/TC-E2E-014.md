## Summary
The PySpark job needs tuning for scale and reliability, especially around partitioning and shuffle behavior.

## Decision
Avoid collect to the driver for large datasets, reduce unnecessary shuffle, and use built-in functions instead of expensive UDF paths when possible.

## Rationale
Driver-heavy operations can cause oom failures, and poor partitioning can amplify shuffle overhead in distributed workloads.

## Trade-offs
| Option | Pros | Cons | When to Use |
|--------|------|------|-------------|
| Built-in transformations | Better optimizer support | Refactor effort | Common Spark transforms |
| UDF-heavy approach | Flexible custom logic | Slower execution | Niche logic only |
